Replay Features at a Glance

We provide the most robust replay system on the market.

Comprehensive Coverage

With up to 12 cameras surrounding the competition, officials have multiple angles to review every play in high definition. Connect broadcast cameras and increase coverage with WingWarp cameras which are remote-controlled.

Inexpensive installation

Installation takes less than one day. Our wireless connections eliminate long, expensive cable runs. If the arena is already wired, our cameras can connect directly to the network. Schools can mount these cameras on existing poles, rails, walls, ceilings and rafters.

Simple game-day setup

1 Plugin Macbook Pro, 2 connect broadcast cameras to network equipment, 3 open instant replay app. Start game.

Easy to use/Training

The system is easy to learn. We recommend that you train several people to use the system at each school. The app clearly shows camera positions and quickly pulls up different views.

Fast Access

Built for speed. The replay app can jump anywhere in the game. The app clearly labels available cameras and quickly rewinds/fast forwards through video.

Zoom

Zoom-in on the play using MacBook’s pinch gesture.

Synchronized playback

All video views move in concert. Every angle always shows the same moment in time. Simply switch between views to see other angles or watch up to four angles simultaneously.

Scoreboard Reader

The replay app connects to and reads data from scoreboard controllers. It annotates the video with scores and times. Supported scoreboards: Daktronics All Sports (serial), Eversan (serial), OES (serial), Nevco (wireless). Connections for other scoreboards may require purchase from 3rd party vendor.

StatCrew Connect

The replay app also connects to StatCrew to record penalty and game statistics automatically. Minimizing data usually recorded by replay technicians.

Play marking

The user of the replay app can mark important times in the video and detail all reviews. The app keeps a list of all the marks/annotations and the user always can jump back to see them again.

Real-time review emails

Immediately after a video review, an email is sent out to designated conference staff and coordinator with information about the play, whether the call was overturned and video from all the camera angles (with the reviewed angles highlighted).

Web site and archive

After each game, all videos and marks/annotations are uploaded to WingWarp’s website. Users can review the entire game or specific plays. Officials’ coordinators can use this website to document refereeing mechanics and train referees.
